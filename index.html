About Me

I am a Year 3 Computer Science undergraduate in the College of Computing and Data Science at Nanyang Technological University. I currently hold a CGPA of 4.78/5.00 and am on track for Highest Distinction. I also achieved a Graduate Record Examination score of 324/340, including 168 in Quantitative Reasoning.

My research interests lie at the intersection of visual analytics, human–AI collaboration, intelligent data systems, and algorithms for complex data exploration. Through NTU's URECA programme under Prof. Yong Wang, I have contributed to research on hierarchical data-fact exploration, interactive outlier analysis, and steerable LLM-driven program evolution. This work has led to FOX, accepted at IEEE VIS 2026; EvoMaestro, conditionally accepted at ACM UIST 2026; and DFCollection, currently under revision.

I am currently broadening this work through the HKUST Summer Research Program under Prof. Raymond Chi-Wing Wong, where I study shortest-path computation and indexing on Riemannian spaces as well as schema-tree compression for natural-language querying of NoSQL databases. I am also developing a factor-mining visual analytics system in collaboration with Fudan University. Alongside research, my professional internship at Centricore gave me experience building and maintaining full-stack enterprise software. I am interested in pursuing PhD research in visual analytics, human-computer interaction, and AI-assisted data analysis, and I welcome opportunities to connect with prospective supervisors and research collaborators.

Projects

Riemannian Space Shortest-Path Simulation

Developed an interactive system for computing and simulating shortest paths on discretized Riemannian spaces. The system compares non-indexed and indexed approaches, including Dijkstra, ALT, Contraction Hierarchies (CH), Hub Labeling, and H2H. This project was completed under the supervision of Prof. Raymond Chi-Wing Wong.

View project

Schema Tree Compression

Built an offline schema-tree compression workflow for 11 datasets, producing compact schema representations that can fit within prompt token budgets for natural-language querying of NoSQL databases. This project was completed under the supervision of Prof. Raymond Chi-Wing Wong.
